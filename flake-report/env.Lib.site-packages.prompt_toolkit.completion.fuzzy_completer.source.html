17 class FuzzyCompleter(Completer):
18 """
19 Fuzzy completion.
20 This wraps any other completer and turns it into a fuzzy completer.
21  
22 If the list of words is: ["leopard" , "gorilla", "dinosaur", "cat", "bee"]
23 Then trying to complete "oar" would yield "leopard" and "dinosaur", but not
24 the others, because they match the regular expression 'o.*a.*r'.
25 Similar, in another application "djm" could expand to "django_migrations".
26  
27 The results are sorted by relevance, which is defined as the start position
28 and the length of the match.
29  
30 Notice that this is not really a tool to work around spelling mistakes,
31 like what would be possible with difflib. The purpose is rather to have a
32 quicker or more intuitive way to filter the given completions, especially
33 when many completions have a common prefix.
34  
35 Fuzzy algorithm is based on this post:
36 https://blog.amjith.com/fuzzyfinder-in-10-lines-of-python
37  
38 :param completer: A :class:`~.Completer` instance.
39 :param WORD: When True, use WORD characters.
40 :param pattern: Regex pattern which selects the characters before the
41 cursor that are considered for the fuzzy matching.
42 :param enable_fuzzy: (bool or `Filter`) Enabled the fuzzy behavior. For
43 easily turning fuzzyness on or off according to a certain condition.
44 """
